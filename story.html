My Story — Catherine Kabiru

My name is Catherine Kabiru. I was born and raised in one of Kenya’s oldest informal settlements — Mathare. Home to more than 200,000 people, Mathare is full of strength, dreams, and community spirit, but also pain and fear. For years, young people here have faced constant police harassment. Many of them — my friends, my neighbors — have been taken away in the middle of the night, never to return.

The statistics are heartbreaking. According to Human Rights Watch and the Independent Policing Oversight Authority (IPOA), Kenya records hundreds of cases of police killings and disappearances every year. In Mathare alone, over 100 young lives have been lost to unlawful police actions between 2017 and 2024. Many victims are found in morgues, bodies lifeless and stories untold.

I created Voice for Justice to make sure these stories are not forgotten — to give people a safe way to record, speak, and be heard. Every voice matters. Every recording we make is a step toward truth and justice for our communities — in Mathare, Dandora, Kayole, Kangemi, and Kawangware.

This project is not just about remembering those we’ve lost. It’s about hope — that one day, young people will walk freely in their neighborhoods, that mothers will no longer fear for their sons, and that Kenya will finally become a nation where justice serves everyone equally.

Our Impact So Far

  • Over 250 voices recorded since launch
  • Collaborations with 4 local organizations in Mathare and Dandora
  • Plans to establish safe audio-reporting centers in informal settlements
